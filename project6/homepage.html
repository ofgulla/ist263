Best Practices for Running a Marathon

How to Train for a Marathon

How exciting it is to run a marathon! As you know, a marathon is 26.2 miles, which is a very long distance to run. To prepare, there are a few main steps a runner needs to follow. First, you must gradually build up mileage so that you prevent injuries and prepare your body for the race. I recommend starting early, so that you are not jumping into long distances right away and can work your way up to the distance. The most common injuries may come from prepping and rushing your preparation too fast so that your body is not prepared. One way to get yourself started which is something I personally have done is to run shorter races like a 5k or 10k. Essentially, the best way to track out your mileage is to follow the calendar I have attached below. This will help slowly increase your mileage and get you on the right track. Every week will include a long run to help build to the marathon distance. I recommend a 12-week training plan to help prepare. Also, there are many steps on how to fuel properly and the advice for the day of the race which will be discussed later.
