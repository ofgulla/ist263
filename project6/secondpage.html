Increasing Mileage

Shown above, is a calendar of work outs for the twelve weeks. There are included rest days in this layout, which means that you need to let your body recover and rest. On rest days, it is great to go for a walk or do yoga, but nothing too straining or high intensity. As the marathon approaches, you will see that mileage begins to slow down to let your body recover and be ready for the day of the race. Each week will have four runs included. At the end of the week, it is recommended to do your long run so that you can recover the day after. The first week will include a ten mile long run and will increase to twenty miles by week 9. For the last three weeks, the long runs will tamper down so that your body can rest and be ready for race day. This plan will help prepare you for the marathon slowly but effectively. Feel free to cross train on the other days, just remember to save your energy for the runs.
