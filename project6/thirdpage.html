Ways To Fuel

You have now reached all your marathon training and are ready for the race! While completing a marathon, you are going to burn a lot of calories and sweat out a bunch of water! The most important thing to do in preparing for a marathon is to always stay hydrated! What you eat the week before a marathon is very important and will influence how you do in the race. The night before the race, make sure to drink plenty of water and eat a high carbohydrate meal with protein. The morning of you should also be consuming a meal with carbohydrates although not too much where you will develop a cramp or upset your stomach. This meal should be several hours before the race and could include toast, a banana, oatmeal, or a bar. Throughout the marathon, there will be water stations that you can stop at to stay hydrated. You also should bring and run with a fanny pack that can hold water. Also, you should bring energy gels or a bar to consume during your marathon. You need to eat something during it to sustain your pace for your run. I recommend trying these energy gels during your prep so that your body is used to digesting them and you will know if they work for you or not. Keep in mind to not try anything new the week leading up to the marathon for you do not want to risk an upset stomach. Listen to your body and fuel it with what you know works best. Make sure to not overdress for the race, because once it starts you do not want to have to hold clothing around your waist and your body will get warm. Try listening to a podcast or a show while you run, to think and get your mind off how much distance you have left to achieve. Also, try to mentally break the race into blocks so that you are not thinking about the overall distance as one.
